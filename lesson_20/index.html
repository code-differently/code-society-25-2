Amazing Orca Facts

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are actually the largest members of the dolphin family and can live up to 90 years in the wild.

Diet & Hunting Behavior

Orcas are apex predators that hunt in coordinated pods and have diverse diets including fish, seals, and even other whales depending on their population.

Conservation Status

While orcas are not globally endangered, some populations face threats from pollution, boat traffic, and declining food sources, making conservation efforts crucial.

Orca Physical Characteristics

+

Orcas are the largest members of the dolphin family. Adult males can reach up to 32 feet in length and weigh up to 6 tons. They have distinctive black and white coloration with white patches behind their eyes.

Orca Behavior and Intelligence

+

Orcas are highly intelligent marine mammals with complex social structures. They live in family groups called pods and have been observed using tools, teaching their young, and even displaying cultural behaviors that vary between populations.

Orca Habitat and Distribution

+

Orcas are found in oceans worldwide, from the Arctic to the Antarctic. They prefer deeper waters but can also be found in coastal areas. Different populations have adapted to different environments and prey.
